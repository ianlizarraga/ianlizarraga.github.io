Welcome! I'm a postdoctoral fellow working with Martin Wechselberger at the University of Sydney.

I'm interested in multiple-timescale dynamical systems having nonstandard timescale splitting, which are used to model a wide variety of physical problems ranging from chemical reaction networks to mechanical stick-slip oscillators. In a recent project, we studied the geometric content underlying a particular approximation algorithm, the computational singular perturbation (CSP) method, which is used to approximate invariant manifolds and fast fiber bundles in these systems.

I'm currently developing coordinate-free approaches to classify and algorithmically detect contact singularities and bifurcations in these systems. I am also working with Theo Vo at the University of Monash to develop the theory of torus canards in ODE systems of arbitrary dimension. My work uses a variety of tools, ranging from applied bifurcation theory and differential geometry to numerical analysis and computational algebraic geometry.

Previously I was a PhD student at Cornell University working with John Guckenheimer on multiple-timescale dynamical systems, and with Steven Strogatz on model reduction in coupled oscillator problems.
